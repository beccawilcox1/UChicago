The University of Chicago


Computational Neuroscience

When researching college majors, I was torn between computer science, biochemistry, and neuroscience. Finding UChicago's computational neuroscience minor was like finding a hidden gem among identical pebbles. Along with a major in computer science, this combination would prepare me for graduate studies, and eventually, research in bioinformatics, quantum computing, or artificial intelligence

NMPDRC

As the home of the National Microbial Pathogen Data Resource Center, UChicago would provide amazing access to internship opportunities. The lab uses bioinformatics to analyze pathogenicity, drug resistance, and infectivity, pertaining directly to my interest in computational neuroscience. Gaining experience in research as an undergrad would allow for me to explore future career possibilities and enrich my education.

Quirkiness

From the famous application essay to the annual scavenger hunt, UChicago embraces the quirky nature of its students. Even the campus itself has character, such as the Mansueto Library with retrival robots and an abnormal structure. Studying in such an environment would allow me to live genuinely, finding my niche among a community of bright, innovative students
